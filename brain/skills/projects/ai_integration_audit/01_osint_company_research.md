# OSINT Company Research (Pre-Intake)

## Why This Exists

If you start intake with generic questions (“what do you sell?”, “who are your customers?”), you waste time and reduce confidence.

This skill defines a **public research-first** workflow that produces a cited company dossier, plus a list of *remaining unknowns* that justify what you ask next.

## When to Use It

Use this before sending any survey or scheduling a discovery call.

## Details

### Research Pattern (use this explicitly)

Follow `skills/domains/code-quality/research-patterns.md`:

- Define the question (what must we know to propose AI opportunities?)
- Time-box the research (e.g., 60–120 minutes for first pass)
- Use **triangulation** (2–3 sources for key claims)
- Apply **CRAAP** quickly to each source
- Synthesize and record confidence level + links

### Output: Company Dossier (minimum viable)

Produce a dossier with:

1. **Facts (high confidence):**
   - What the company sells (offers, pricing tiers, packages)
   - Who it sells to (ICP, segments, geographies)
   - Channels (inbound/outbound, paid, partnerships)
   - Operating footprint (locations, hours, service areas)

2. **Signals (medium confidence):**
   - Likely tools (detected via tech signals + job posts)
   - Likely workflows (support, sales, operations)
   - Likely constraints (regulated industry, procurement language)

3. **Hypotheses to validate (explicitly labeled):**
   - “Support volume is high”
   - “Lead qualification is manual”
   - “Content is a bottleneck”

4. **Unknowns (survey/call targets):**
   - Volumes (tickets/week, leads/week)
   - Exception paths (refunds, escalations, approvals)
   - Access constraints (security, data retention)

5. **Sources (traceability):**
   - URLs + date accessed
   - Notes on source reliability

### Source Checklist (starter set)

Prioritize sources in this order:

1. Company website: homepage, product/service pages, pricing, case studies, FAQs
2. Security/privacy pages: GDPR/SOC2/HIPAA claims (verify)
3. Job postings: tooling, process maturity, pain points, KPIs
4. Public reviews: Google, G2, Capterra, Trustpilot (look for repeated pain)
5. Social/press: LinkedIn posts, podcasts, interviews
6. Support portal/knowledge base (if public)
7. Competitors’ positioning (to infer category expectations)

### “Don’t Ask These” List

From the dossier, generate a list of questions to avoid:

- Any fact already verified publicly
- Any question answered by the company’s pricing/FAQ pages

Replace them with:

- Confirmation questions (“Is this still accurate internally?”)
- Quantification questions (“How many/week?”)
- Constraint questions (“Who must approve changes?”)

### Red Flags / Failure Modes

- Over-trusting tool detection (pixels ≠ usage maturity)
- Treating marketing claims as operational truth
- Not recording sources (no traceability → weak recommendations)

## Deliverables

Done when you have:

- A dossier document (facts + hypotheses + unknowns)
- A short list of likely quick-win opportunities (to validate)
- A survey outline generated from unknowns
